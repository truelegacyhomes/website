Our Promise to You

The Legacy Assurance Plan

We know you've heard horror stories about estate sales gone wrong. That's why we put our promises in writing—so you know exactly what to expect from us.

Your Rights as Our Client

Many families expect the worst when selling a home's contents—and honestly, that's often what they get with other companies. We're changing that. The Legacy Assurance Plan is our commitment to you: a clear set of rights you can hold us to, regardless of whether you work with us or someone else.

1

The Right to Dignity

Your family's personal property and home will be treated with the highest level of respect throughout every stage of the process. These aren't just "things" to us—they're pieces of someone's life, and we honor that.

2

The Right to a Clear Timeline

You'll receive a realistic timeframe that we stand by—no vague promises. We'll communicate specifically what activities will take place in your home, and when, so you always know the "what and when" throughout the entire process.

3

The Right to Be Heard

You can express your thoughts, concerns, or compliments freely and openly throughout the entire process—and you'll actually be heard. We want to know how you're feeling, not just what you need done.

4

The Right to Fair Problem-Solving

When challenges arise (and they sometimes do), our entire team is committed to effective, honest, and economically fair solutions. We work to make the process as seamless and painless as possible—never at your expense.

5

The Right to Safety & Security

Your home and everything inside it will be treated with utmost care and cleanliness during the entire estate sale process. You'll know each day who will be in your home and when they'll arrive or depart. Your home and your family's belongings will be safe.

6

The Right to "Owner-Level" Care

Every team member at True Legacy Homes will treat your estate sale, your home, and its contents like it was our own. This means organizing every item, researching unique pieces with professional pricing databases and appraisers, and leaving your home completely empty and broom clean when we're done.

7

The Right to Keep What Matters

You and your family can remove any family treasures, valuables, collectibles, antiques—literally anything you want to keep from the home. We also promise to share any items of interest we discover during our staging process with you. If we find grandma's wedding album tucked in a closet, you'll know about it.
