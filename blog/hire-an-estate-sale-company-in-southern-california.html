7 Essential Steps to Hire an Estate Sale Company in Southern California


Hiring an estate sale company in Southern California involves strategic planning to maximize returns and minimize stress during transitions such as downsizing or asset liquidation. This guide outlines seven essential steps to find and hire the best company for your needs.

Step 1: Define your goals, constraints, and timeline

A successful estate sale begins with clearly defining your objectives, limitations, and timeline.

  • Goals: Identify whether you aim to maximize profit from valuable items, require quick liquidation, or wish to preserve family heirlooms while generating income.
  • Constraints: Budget limitations may affect service levels, while timeframes can create pressure for quick transitions.
  • Timeline: Establish a realistic schedule, accounting for preparation (2-4 weeks), marketing, and the sale itself. A well-planned timeline reduces stress and enhances outcomes.

Step 2: Preserve items before the walkthrough

Safeguarding valuable and sentimental items before the initial assessment is crucial.

  • Inventory: Create a list of items to exclude, such as heirlooms, personal documents, jewelry, and promised items.
  • Preservation: Use professional cleaning for artwork, secure storage for documents, and insurance for valuables. Complete necessary restoration before the walkthrough.
  • Communication: Clearly mark preserved items and inform the estate sale company to avoid misunderstandings.

Step 3: Research and vet potential estate sale companies

Thorough research and vetting protect your interests and ensure you select a capable team.

  • Referrals: Seek recommendations from real estate agents, attorneys, and friends. Consider companies like True Legacy Homes and consult directories like the American Society of Estate Liquidators.
  • Credentials: Check licensing, bonding, and insurance. Review online feedback, BBB ratings, and testimonials.
  • Specialization: Choose companies with expertise relevant to your items and familiarity with Southern California’s market.
  • Marketing: Assess their online presence, social media, and advertising strategies.
  • References: Verify experiences from recent clients for insights into the company’s professionalism and results.

Step 4: Schedule walkthroughs and consultations

Walkthroughs allow you to assess companies while they gather information for accurate proposals.

  • Selection: Schedule walkthroughs with 3-5 companies for thorough evaluation.
  • Observation: Note the representative’s professionalism, expertise, and communication style.
  • Questions: Prepare inquiries about commission, marketing strategies, sale duration, and unsold item policies.
  • Documentation: Record responses and proposals for easy comparison. Proposals should detail services, timelines, and fees.

Step 5: Compare proposals and select your company

Carefully comparing proposals ensures you choose the best partner for your goals.

  • Framework: Create a comparison system evaluating commission rates, services, and marketing strategies.
  • Scope of Services: Comprehensive packages usually yield better results, including item research, staging, and cleanup.
  • Marketing Impact: Strong marketing strategies affect attendance and sales.
  • Timeline: Ensure proposed schedules meet your constraints.
  • Contract Review: Examine terms, focusing on commission structure, expenses, and dispute handling.

Select based on expertise, service quality, and overall value rather than price alone.

Step 6: Finalize contracts and logistics

Proper contract and logistics management set clear expectations for a smooth execution.

  • Contract Review: Confirm clarity on commission rates, included items, services, and liability protection.
  • Communication Protocols: Establish a primary contact and preferred methods for updates.
  • Logistics Coordination: Ensure access, security, and utilities are arranged for the estate sale company.
  • Unsold Item Management: Decide on options for unsold items, such as donations or disposal, and clarify these in the contract.
  • Insurance Verification: Confirm coverage for potential damages during the sale process.

Step 7: Manage the sale and post-sale activities

Active management during the sale ensures optimal results and proper conclusion.

  • Setup Communication: Stay in touch with the company while respecting their expertise.
  • Monitor Progress: Attend the sale briefly if necessary, but allow professionals to handle negotiations.
  • Sales Tracking: Review daily reports of transactions to monitor success and identify discrepancies.
  • Final Accounting Review: Verify sold items documentation and commission calculations after the sale.
  • Final Walkthrough: Ensure cleanup and handling of remaining items aligns with prior arrangements.

Provide feedback to the estate sale company about your experience to help them improve services.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do estate sale companies typically charge in Southern California?

Most estate sale companies charge 25–40% of total sales, depending on the size of the estate and the value of the items. Always clarify what’s included in the commission—such as marketing, staffing, and cleanup—and whether there are any hidden or additional fees before signing your contract.

How long does the entire estate sale process take from start to finish?

Expect the full process to take about 3–6 weeks. This includes research, preparation, pricing, marketing, the actual sale, and post-sale cleanup. Larger estates or complicated schedules can extend that timeline, so plan with flexibility.

What happens to items that don’t sell during the estate sale?

Unsold items don’t have to be a headache. Most companies offer donation, consignment, markdown sales, or disposal options depending on your goals. Discuss these plans upfront to ensure your expectations match their services.

Do I need to be present during the estate sale?

Usually, no—and many companies actually recommend staying away during selling hours. This helps reduce emotional stress and allows professionals to focus on sales. You can, however, stop by during setup to answer last-minute questions or confirm arrangements.

How do I prepare my home for an estate sale company’s walkthrough?

Before the walkthrough, remove any personal or sentimental items you want to keep. Make sure every room is accessible and clutter-free for easy evaluation. You don’t need to deep clean—just create a safe, organized space and gather documents or appraisals for high-value pieces.
