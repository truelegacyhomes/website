Why are we seeing more estate sales?

There are two primary causes for an estate sale. The first is when the owner of a property passes away. Often, the kin will host an estate sale to pass on any belongings that the friends and family do not wish to keep. This clears out the house so that it is ready to be sold. The second reason people frequently host estate sales is that they are making a big move and cannot take most of their belongings with them. It is much easier to sell stuff when you do not have to move it! The pandemic ignited a surge in home buying that we haven’t seen since 2006. A rise in remote work opportunities, a desire to relocate to less densely populated areas, and a large percentage of the population entering retirement are just some of the reasons people are on the move. (1) An estate sale turns a house into a shopping experience for potential buyers, and it is our job here at True Legacy to optimize that space so you can sell as many belongings as possible.

What should I look for in a good estate sale planner?

You may want to look for several things in an estate sale planner. You want them to be reliable and trustworthy. When working in estate sales, you are handling many items, some of which could be extremely valuable. After the sale, you are handling potentially large sums of money. You want this person to be transparent and someone that you can trust to handle the money. It is important to consider what you are looking for in an estate sale planner before you begin searching. That way, you can know what to look for when you begin your search. 

What is the difference between an estate sale and a garage?

This is a question that we see appear quite frequently! When you think of a garage sale, you think of a Saturday in May after all of the spring cleaning and closet purging has come to an end. You think of families setting up outside their garages with old clothes and children’s toys. You think of small children running their lemonade stand, hoping to make a few dollars in spare change. These are either neighborhood-organized or individually organized-events that sell the items you do not want to store anymore. 

An estate sale is essentially a garage sale on steroids. Rather than selling your children’s clothes from the previous year and the toys they have not touched since you painstakingly removed them from the box, you are selling the entire contents of a property. This could be anything from clothing to a refrigerator to trinkets to jewelry to furniture. If it is in a house, it can be sold. Of course, you can remove items that you wish to keep, but for the most part, everything is up for grabs!
