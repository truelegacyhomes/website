Frequently Asked Questions

Are estate sale companies licensed or regulated in California?

California does not issue a specific estate sale license. Most reputable companies, including True Legacy Homes, operate as bonded and insured businesses and may hold local business permits or auctioneer licenses. Always verify credentials before hiring.

Do I need permits or HOA approval to host a sale in San Diego?

Yes, San Diego homeowners often need HOA approval, and some neighborhoods require city permits for estate sales. True Legacy Homes assists clients by reviewing HOA bylaws and handling permit requirements to ensure compliance before the sale.

Who handles sales tax for a San Diego estate sale?

Estate sale companies are responsible for collecting and remitting sales tax to the California Department of Tax and Fee Administration (CDTFA). True Legacy Homes manages this process directly, so families do not have to handle tax filings themselves.

Can I manage an estate sale remotely if I am out of the area?

Yes, estate sales can be managed remotely with the right provider. True Legacy Homes offers digital contracts, video consultations, and real-time reporting so families outside San Diego can oversee the process without traveling.

What items cannot be sold at an estate sale in California?

Prohibited items include hazardous materials, recalled products, unlicensed firearms, prescription drugs, and perishable food. True Legacy Homes helps clients identify restricted items and arranges safe donation or disposal through approved channels.

When is a buyout better than a traditional estate sale?

A buyout is best when speed is critical, such as preparing a home for a quick sale or clearing property within days. While profits are typically lower, True Legacy Homes can facilitate both traditional sales and buyouts, helping families choose the most practical option.
